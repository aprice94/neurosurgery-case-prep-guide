# 🧭 Approach Selection — Decision Aids

Quick, chief-level **"which corridor for which lesion"** comparison tables. These summarize the *typical* trade-offs; the final choice always depends on the individual patient, lesion anatomy, surgeon experience, and institutional practice. Each option links to its detailed [approach chapter](../approaches/) or [case guide](../../).

> ⚠️ Decision support only — not a substitute for individualized judgment, imaging review, and multidisciplinary discussion.

---

## Anterior-circulation aneurysm

| Approach | Best for | Key advantage | Main risks / limits | Consider instead |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Pterional](pterional-craniotomy.md) | MCA, ICA, AComA, PComA | Workhorse; flexible; fast | Temporalis atrophy, frontalis palsy | OZ for high/giant |
| [Orbitozygomatic](orbitozygomatic-craniotomy.md) | High/large basilar apex, giant ICA, deep parasellar | Maximal upward/basal angle, less brain retraction | Cosmetic/orbital morbidity | Pterional if angle not needed |
| [Supraorbital "eyebrow" keyhole](supraorbital-keyhole-craniotomy.md) | Favorable AComA, small subfrontal/suprasellar | Minimal, cosmetic | Limited proximal control; frontal-sinus | Pterional if complex |
| **Endovascular** ([coiling/flow diversion](../endovascular/aneurysm-coiling-flow-diversion.md)) | Posterior circulation, poor-grade SAH, elderly, wide-neck (stent/FD) | No craniotomy | Retreatment, antiplatelets (FD) | Clip if MCA/wide-neck/mass effect |

## Sellar / suprasellar

| Approach | Best for | Advantage | Limits |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Endoscopic endonasal](endoscopic-endonasal-approach.md) | Pituitary adenoma, midline tuberculum/planum, clival | No brain retraction; direct ventral | CSF leak; lateral reach |
| [Supraorbital keyhole](supraorbital-keyhole-craniotomy.md) | Small midline suprasellar, selected tuberculum | Minimal | Narrow corridor |
| [Pterional/OZ](pterional-craniotomy.md) | Lateral extension, vascular encasement, large | Wide, proximal vascular control | Brain retraction, optic manipulation |
| [Bifrontal](bifrontal-craniotomy.md) | Large midline olfactory groove/planum | Bilateral exposure + pericranial reconstruction | Anosmia, frontal retraction |

## Cerebellopontine angle & lateral posterior fossa

| Approach | Best for | Hearing | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Retrosigmoid](retrosigmoid-craniotomy.md) | VS, CPA meningioma/epidermoid, MVD | Preservable | Workhorse, any size |
| Translabyrinthine | VS with non-serviceable hearing | Sacrificed | Direct IAC, no cerebellar retraction |
| [Presigmoid / petrosal](presigmoid-petrosal-approach.md) | Large petroclival | Per variant | Big exposure, morbid |
| [Far-lateral](far-lateral-craniotomy.md) | Foramen magnum, VA/PICA, lower CN | — | Ventral CCJ |

## Petroclival lesion

| Approach | Reach | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|
| [Retrosigmoid (± suprameatal)](retrosigmoid-craniotomy.md) | Mid-posterior petrous face | Least morbid; limited ventral/upper |
| [Subtemporal + anterior petrosectomy (Kawase)](subtemporal-craniotomy.md) | Upper clivus, ventral pons | Temporal lobe/vein of Labbé |
| [Combined petrosal](presigmoid-petrosal-approach.md) | Whole clivus | Most exposure; hearing/facial, CSF leak, time |

## Fourth ventricle / pineal

| Approach | Best for | Advantage |
|---|---|---|
| [Telovelar](telovelar-approach.md) | Floor-to-aqueduct 4th-ventricle tumors | Vermis-sparing (less mutism) |
| [Supracerebellar-infratentorial](supracerebellar-infratentorial-approach.md) | Pineal region, posterior 3rd ventricle | Midline, gravity retraction |
| [Midline suboccipital](midline-suboccipital-craniotomy.md) | Posterior fossa exposure / Chiari | The bony approach the above use |

## Cervical degenerative (myelopathy / radiculopathy)

| Approach | Best for | Avoid when |
|---|---|---|
| [Anterior (ACDF / arthroplasty)](anterior-cervical-approach.md) | 1–3 level ventral disease, kyphosis | Multilevel + ossified PLL spanning many levels |
| [Posterior (laminoplasty / laminectomy+fusion)](posterior-cervical-approach.md) | Multilevel, **lordotic** alignment, OPLL | **Fixed kyphosis** (cord won't drift back) |

## Lumbar interbody fusion

| Option | Corridor | Best for | Watch |
|---|---|---|---|
| [TLIF](../spine-degenerative/tlif.md) | Posterior (Kambin/facetectomy) | 1–2 level, posterior decompression needed | Same-side root |
| PLIF | Posterior bilateral | — | More retraction |
| [ALIF](../spine-degenerative/alif.md) | Anterior retroperitoneal | L5-S1/L4-5, big lordosis | Great vessels, retrograde ejaculation |
| [LLIF / OLIF (transpsoas)](transpsoas-approach.md) | Lateral | L1-2→L4-5, indirect decompression, coronal correction | Lumbar plexus (L4-5); **not L5-S1** |

## Thoracic disc / anterior column

| Approach | Best for | Key point |
|---|---|---|
| Transpedicular / costotransversectomy | Lateral/paracentral soft disc | Posterolateral; no cord retraction |
| Lateral extracavitary / mini-open lateral | Ventral disc/corpectomy, avoid chest cavity | Retropleural |
| [Transthoracic (open/VATS)](transthoracic-approach.md) | Central calcified disc, corpectomy, tumor | Direct ventral; lung isolation, Adamkiewicz |
| ⚠️ **Never** a simple posterior laminectomy for a central thoracic disc | — | Cord retraction = catastrophe |

## History of Present Illness
- Chief complaint: Head trauma with scalp laceration / focal deficit / seizure
- Mechanism: blunt focal impact, assault, fall, projectile
- **Open vs closed:** open (compound) = scalp laceration communicating with fracture → infection risk → urgent surgery
- GCS, focal deficits, seizure, CSF leak from wound

## Surgical Planning

### Diagnosis & Indication
- Indication: Open depressed fracture (urgent — debride, prevent infection), depression > thickness of skull, underlying mass lesion, dural laceration/CSF leak, gross cosmetic deformity, frontal sinus involvement
- Closed depressed fracture without dural breach/deficit can sometimes be managed conservatively
- **Caution:** depressed fracture over a major venous sinus — elevation may cause torrential bleeding (prep, blood available, consider leaving if asymptomatic)

### Position
- Per location, Mayfield or horseshoe; head positioned with fracture up

### Key Surgical Steps
1. Debride scalp laceration (open), excise devitalized tissue
2. Craniotomy/burr hole placed in NORMAL bone adjacent to the depressed segment (not through fragments)
3. Elevate depressed fragments (from the adjacent craniotomy, lift fragments)
4. **Inspect dura:** repair lacerations (watertight, dural graft if needed), evacuate underlying hematoma, debride contused brain/foreign material (open injuries)
5. Irrigate copiously (open — remove debris/contaminants)
6. **Sinus involvement:** control bleeding (Gelfoam, packing, repair); may leave fragment over patent sinus if removal risks exsanguination
7. Reconstruct: replace bone fragments (if clean/closed) or delayed cranioplasty (contaminated/comminuted); titanium mesh/plates
8. Frontal sinus: cranialize or obliterate if involved
9. Closure

### Critical Anatomy & Structures at Risk
1. **Dura and underlying cortex** — laceration, contusion
2. **Dural venous sinuses** (sagittal, transverse) — major bleeding
3. **Frontal sinus** (anterior fractures) — mucocele/infection if not addressed
4. Cosmesis

### Equipment
- [ ] Craniotome, drill, elevators
- [ ] Dural substitute, sealant
- [ ] Cranial fixation (plates/mesh), hemostatic agents
- [ ] Copious irrigation, debridement instruments

### Anesthesia
- [ ] Antibiotics (broad if open), tetanus, blood available (sinus), seizure prophylaxis

### Potential Complications
1. Infection (open injuries) — debridement, antibiotics, delayed cranioplasty
2. Sinus bleeding/thrombosis
3. Seizures (cortical injury)
4. CSF leak, cosmetic deformity, frontal sinus mucocele
